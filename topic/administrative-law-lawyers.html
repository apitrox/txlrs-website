Trusted Houston Administrative Law Lawyers and Attorneys

Administrative law is the body of law created by the agencies and departments of the government, which carry out the laws passed by Congress or a state legislature. Passing laws on difficult issues require Congress to determine how the new law will be rolled out. Administrative law agencies and the government may adopt additional rules and regulations to achieve Congress's goals. Houston administrative law lawyers can guide you further with regulatory law matters.

People often deal with administrative agencies and administrative law when they apply for government benefits. For example, Congress has passed laws that allow disabled individuals to receive government assistance. The Social Security Administration (SSA) is the administrative agency created to implement Congress's social security and disability laws. The SSA receives applications when people apply for disability benefits and determines who is eligible for the benefits. Then it passes rules and regulations to ensure that only the people who deserve these benefits receive them.

In addition to regulating government benefits like Social Security, administrative agencies also implement federal and state laws affecting almost every industry. For example, government bodies like the Department of Labor and the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) create and enforce workplace safety regulations. The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) passes rules and rules to implement Congress's goal of protecting the environment. States also have their administrative agencies to implement and execute laws passed by their state legislatures.

Why Do People Need to Hire Administrative Lawyers in Texas?

People need to hire administrative lawyers when an administrative agency denies their applications for benefits or imposes penalties for failing to comply with regulations. Important Considerations When Hiring Houston Administrative Law Lawyers

The federal government contains more than 100 administrative agencies covering a variety of topics. These organizations are devoted to transportation safety, health care, homeland security, and environmental conservation. Because each agency has its own distinct rules, regulations, and procedures, it is essential that you contact a specialized attorney who has experience handling your type of legal matters.

Rules and regulations passed by administrative agencies are not the same as laws passed by Congress, or a state Legislature. Furthermore, they may carry similar penalties if you do not obey the rules. Many administrative organizations have the power to fine individuals and corporations that fail to comply with administrative regulations.
