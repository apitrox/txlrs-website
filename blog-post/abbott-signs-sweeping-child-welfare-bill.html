Landmark Child Welfare Reform in Texas

Governor Greg Abbott signed landmark legislation that fundamentally changed how the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) protects children across the state. These sweeping reforms addressed critical issues within the Texas child welfare system that had been putting vulnerable children at risk.

The legislation came after months of advocacy from state leaders and widespread media coverage highlighting serious problems within the child welfare program. Reports revealed insufficient resources, children sleeping in state offices, and inadequate living conditions for children in state care.

Four Key Child Welfare Bills Signed Into Law

Governor Abbott signed four critical bills at the signing ceremony, each addressing different aspects of child welfare in Texas:

  • House Bill 4: Allocates $350 per month to families caring for abused and neglected children, including relatives and kinship placements
  • House Bill 5: Establishes the Department of Family and Protective Services as a separate, independent agency outside of the Texas Health and Human Services Commission
  • Senate Bill 7: Implements important changes to how Texas courts work with the state's child welfare agency
  • Senate Bill 11: Creates a "community-based care" model allowing non-profit organizations to assist children in foster care and adoption

Why These Child Welfare Reforms Matter to Texas Families

Understanding the challenges within the Texas child welfare system is vital for all Texas families. These reforms directly impact thousands of families and children across the state. Whether you're dealing with a CPS investigation, fighting for custody of a family member's child, or navigating the foster care system, these laws affect your rights and options.

The children of Texas deserve the strongest protections. These reforms represent a significant step toward balancing government assistance with the sanctity of the family unit.
